Getting stuff done is hard. Procrastination gets in the way. Hubado offers a new combination of several old and proven approaches to help you with all kinds of goals. We will keep it engaging, fun and social. You engage by betting your money on your commitment, and then you join a randomly chosen group of hubado-ers (via online conference) to state what you are going to accomplish today, as well as how far you've progressed since your last hubup (a standup call in the hubado world)

We merge two concepts: one from the "Commitment Contract" as described by Dean Karlan (Professor of Economics at Yale University) here; and another from one of the key components of scrum software development methodology: the standup meeting. Extensive research has proven that a monetory wager creates a much stronger commitment bond than other types of commitments (e.g. New Years Resolutions). At the same time software development industry continues to adopt agile practices, and even the late adopters first and foremost go for the standup meeting, because it quickly reinforces the commitment for "today" and makes whatever was accomplished (or not) yesterday publicly known.
